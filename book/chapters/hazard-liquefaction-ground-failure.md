## Scientific framing

Earthquake shaking can cause saturated, loose soils to lose strength and behave as a fluid —
liquefaction — producing settlement, lateral spreading, and ground failure. Susceptibility
depends on the **antecedent hydromechanical state** (saturation, water-table depth) coupled
to the seismic ground-motion field, making it a coupling between the soil reanalysis and
earthquake wavefields.
